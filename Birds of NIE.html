Birds of NTU-NIE

Visitors to my hall window

The ledge outside my hall windows somehow appears to be a great resting spot for many passing birds. Often alerted by their calls, the position allows me to take some really good pictures.

Yellow Vented Bulbul

A common bird found everywhere in Singapore. The Vent refers to it's butt and can be easily identified by its yellow coloration.

Yellow Vented Bulbul's call'

An uncommon visitor to my hall window. I am usually awoken by its distinct chirping call in the mornings.

Black-naped Oriole

A common bird found everywhere in Singapore. Easily identified by its bright yellow color and black markings that extend to its nape

Black-naped Oriole's call'

Despite it's bright yellow coloration, this bird is often heard before seen due to its distinct melodious call.'

Birds Around NIE

Birds around NIE(That I could get a picture of)

Asian Glossy Starlings

A common small black bird with distinct red eyes. Looks like a smaller Koel and I used to confuse the two before.

Imperial Pied Pigeon

A beautiful uncommon visitor to NIE, commonly seen in a group.

Sunda Pygmy Woodpecker

The smallest woodpecker in Singapore. Looks absolutely adorable and was lucky enough to catch a video of this one pecking.

Nightjar

A really rare bird. In fact, I didn't even know it wa a nightjar until looking back on it. Pity it was a rainy day with poor lighting.'

Buffy Fish owls

My first owl sightings in Singapore! In broad daylight too! And two of them! They sat at that tree for a whole week allowing me to come to school with my binoculars and take some great shots! These are my best shots till today!

A Baby BulBul

A baby bulbul sighting in NIE! Absolutely estatic for this encounter and I could follow the bulbul's growth throughout the week.(as best as i could)'

Bulbul's Nest'

The baby bulbul had emerged from its nest as it had gotten too big and was running around, learning to fly.

Baby Bulbul running around

It ended up running around a lot in the open. Was really concern as it was amzing how so many people simply walked by it without noticing that they could have kicked or stepped on the poor baby. Said poor baby clearly didn't care.'

Feeding time!

It's parents frequently came back to feed it and I was able to catch it by waiting. The baby made a lot of noise to alert the parent of it's position. This baby took weeks to finally fly off on it's own...talk about smooching off it's parents...
