Common Birds

Yellow Vented Bulbul

A common bird found everywhere in Singapore. The Vent refers to it's butt and can be easily identified by its yellow coloration.

Asian Koel(Female)

That 'uwu' bird that annoys everyone with it's call. Females are brown unlike the black male counterpart but both share red eyes. I actually don't have a good shot of the a male koel...

Black-naped Oriole

A common bird found everywhere in Singapore. Easily identified by its bright yellow color and black markings that extend to its nape

A juvenile Black-naped Oriole

The juvenile actually doesn't have the black nape, throwing me off in identification the first time I saw this bird. Yet, there aren't many yellow birds in Singapore.

Pink Neck Green Pigeon (Male)

A common green bird found everywhere in Singapore. The males have a distinct pink coloration around their neck.

Pink Neck Green Pigeon (Female)

The female counterpart lacks the pink coloration as always in the bird kingdom. The completely green coloration would help it stay more camouflaged in the foliage.

Malaysian Pied Fantail

Actually a common bird found in many forested places around Singapore. It's known for it's tail that opens like a fan, hence it's name. Till today, I have yet to capture a good image of it's fantail though:(

Pin-striped Tit Babbler

A small tiny bird found in many bushes. Makes a whole lotta noise and that's how you find them.

White Collared KingFisher

The most common Kingfisher found in Singapore. It is mostly white and blue. It's name comes from the white 'collar'. This bird is actually a really loud and noisy bird and is mostly heard before seen.

Jungle Fowl

A chicken. This chicken though was white and was therefore interesting enough for me to get a shot of. Reminded me of my white chicken rice.

Uncommon Birds

Birds here are either uncommon in general or found in specific places.

Oriental Magpie Robin

I say uncommon, but at specific places, they can be found everywhere. For example, this one was at the Botanic Gardens.

Leaf Winged Bulbul

Another species of bulbul in Singapore. This one is harder to identify due to it's dull coloration.

Straw Headed Bulbul

This species of bulbul is a very unique species of bulbul to Singapore in that Singapore is the only place where it is not endangered or extinct. It has a very distinct call that I am not familiar with as I have only seen this once:(

Imperial Pied Pigeon

A beautiful pigeon in Singapore that are commonly seen as a group.

Straited Heron

A common water bird that can be even be found in the longkangs of Singapore.

Little Egret

A common water bird that can be found near most water bodies. It can be differentiated from its other egret friends by it's black beak.

Greater? Egret

This egret is too far to be identified by me.

Some flowerpecker (female)

The thing about flowerpeckers is: The females all look the same. Only the males are bright and colorful. So this flowerpecker is unidentified.

White Crested Laughingthrush

Found mainly in Bukit Timah and Bukit Batok parks and reserves. Very distinct white crest but more notable are their calls. I could hear these calls from really far away, making me wonder what sort of commotion was occuring.

Rare Birds

Self-explanatory

Racket Tailed Drongo

This beautiful bird is known for is distinct tail. Two thin elongated outer tail featers extend from it. I had one flown by my face once but I got too excited and missed it:P

Black baza

A migrant raptor that commonly visits Singapore. Saw this at Sungei Buloh and was pointed out kindly by an elderly birdwatching couple.

Juvenile Crested goshawk 3

This was taken at Pasir Ris Park. There were an entire group of birdwatchers looking up but my friends and I couldn't see anything. Not until we went round and came back and a kind man pointed it out to us. We didn't bother waiting for it's parents to come back to take an adult shot:P

Oriental Honey Buzzard

This guy came by when we were distractedly looking at a Black Naped Oriole. We jumped and quickly moved to get shots which gave me a nice motion capture of the buzzard taking off. The buzzard continued flying around the vincity but we could only watch it soar from below.
